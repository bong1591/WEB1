The World Wide Web (abberviated WWW or the Web) is an information space where documents and other web resources are identified by Uniform Resource Locators (URLs), interlinked by hypertext links, and accessible via the Internet. [1] Enghish scientist Tim berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web in 1989. He wrote the first web browser in 1990 while employed at CERN in Switzerland. [2][3] The browser was reeleased outside CERN in 1990 while other research institutions starting in January 1991 and to the general public on the Internet in August 1991.
